My learning plan

This post outlines my learning plan. I will abide by this plan as long as I am studying at DevAcademy.

Long-term goal

Eventually, I hope that I can have my own website, content and design studio. With my experience in written content and user experience, I would aim at small to medium businesses for these reasons:

  • they will find it easier to deal with just one person for all of their digital needs
  • they may have little technological experience and could get ripped off in the marketplace by larger companies who won’t deliver them results
  • they should be able to save on overall costs by coming to me.

I am not opposed to following other career pathways that may turn up. These could include working full-time for a larger firm, getting into cybersecurity or taking on a role with a company that liaises between content creators and web developers.

Strengths and limitations

My strengths as a learner are:

  • I am adaptable and pick up new skills easily
  • when I enjoy something I become passionate and I can put a lot of effort in
  • I am creative and can come up with innovative solutions to problem

My weaknesses are:

  • I am easily distracted and can waste a lot of time this way
  • I can lose motivation towards the end of a project because I like starting things more than finishing them
  • I have poor personal organisation
  • I have bodily pain problems that can make it hard for me to learn when I have flare-ups

Skills to develop

The personal skills I would like to develop are:

  • personal organisation (especially time management)
  • consistency of effort throughout projects
  • being more mindful to manage my response to my pain.

Workload management

  1. During foundations, I will work at the campus more days than I work from home. I am more productive here.
  2. During boot camp, I will be out of bed 1hr30mins before I need to be at campus. This will ensure I am always on time and ready to learn.
  3. I will use the Toggl app to track my time and compare this to the TimeBoxes.
  4. I will complete my reflections for each task immediately after I have done the task.
  5. When I am stuck, I will reach out to my facilitator if I feel frustrated.
  6. I will write down problems in my book so that I can remember them when talking to my facilitator.

My expectations from the facilitators

All I expect from the facilitation team is to maintain the level of quality I have received so far. The level of teaching, support and communication so far has been great.

This looks like:

  • Being reasonably available to respond to my problems. I don’t want to put hard times on this because life can be hectic but I would normally expect to get help within a few hours of reaching out.
  • Being able to explain things in a different way if I don’t understand the course material

Block-out times

I have block-out times that can’t be predicted. I have a weekly appointment at the osteopath that moves around all the time. I also can need to take further time as I have flare-ups with pain or other related medical problems.

Because of this, I need the course to be able to be reasonably flexible if I have to miss time unexpectedly. For my part, I will endeavour to catch up as soon as possible when this happens.
