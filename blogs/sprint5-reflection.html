My Learning Journey

We’ve learned a lot in five weeks. Of course, the learning speed is about to be cranked up to warp nine starting on monday. But, we have already come a long way.

A big part of the learning journey has been the Core curriculum. The non-technical part of the course has been illuminating in several ways. This blog contains my response to the Core curriculum and my journey through it.

Meditation

About five years ago I meditated regularly. Looking back, I can’t remember why I stopped. I got a lot out of it. I felt more relaxed and emotionally stable.

So, when meditation came up as a Core curriculum task I took to it like a fish to water. I began regularly meditating again, just for a few minutes a day.

I’ve started to feel happier and more calm again. It has been great to be reminded of a great practise that I fell out of.

Conscious Listening

I’ve long felt that the modern world doesn’t leave a lot of time for listening. Talking a lot and drawing attention to yourself are highly rewarded while listening can even be criticised.

I’m reminded of the 2016 U.S. presidential election. Donald Trump got the attention of everyone - mostly of people who proclaimed that they hated him, yet spent their time focussing on his antics.

Meanwhile, Hillary Clinton embarked on a “listening tour” in which she toured the country to hear the concerns of the people. This was mostly ignored by the media and criticised as being “soft” when mentioned.

To have listening as an exercise in this course has therefore been refreshing. I enjoyed the conscious listening exercises and look forward to doing more of this in the future.

Overall Impressions

I’ve found the Core curriculum to be very useful. I was familiar with a lot of the concepts from previous work experience and have always tried to incorporate them into my life. To be given a prime opportunity to practise these concepts has been awesome and I look forward to doing more of it in boot camp. I think every tertiary course should have a Core component.
