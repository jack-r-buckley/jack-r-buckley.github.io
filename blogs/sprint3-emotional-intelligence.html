Emotional Intelligence

In this course we have a heavy learning focus on emotional intelligence. Emotional intelligence is all about understanding, managing and using your emotions correctly. It’s also about communicating effectively and empathising with others.

Working on your emotional intelligence has clear benefits to your mental and physical health, relationships and professional success.

Employability Skills

Last year, while working at the Tertiary Education Commission, I worked on resources that provided information to jobseekers about how to get jobs and be employable. There was a heavy focus on what we called ‘employability skills’, which is a part of emotional intelligence.

There is a lot of evidence suggesting that communication, self-awareness and other employability skills are highly sought after by employers - as much or more than technical skills.

I took this with a grain of salt until I started interviewing employers. They confirmed everything the evidence said and then some. So what is emotional intelligence about and how can you improve yours?

The Four Pillars of Emotional Intelligence

Self-management
  • Recognising impulses and not acting on them
  • Making commitments and sticking to them
  • Adapting to changing circumstances
 Self-awareness
  • Recognising your own feelings and emotions
  • Knowing your strengths and weaknesses
  • Having self-confidence, but not arrogance
Social awareness
  • Being empathetic
  • Picking up on social cues and knowing how to act on them
  • Understanding cultural differences
  • Being literate in power dynamics within organisations
 Relationship management
  • Developing and maintain relationships
  • Communicating clearly and effectively
  • Inspiring and influencing others and letting them do the same to you
  • Working well in a team and managing conflict

Why Improve your EQ?

Improved health. If you can’t manage your emotions properly, it can cause stress and contribute to mental illness. This can also affect you physically by causing high blood pressure, insomnia, high blood pressure and more.

Career success. Increasingly, employers are recognising that emotional skills contribute to sustained success in the workplace. A raft of evidence shows that high emotional intelligence is correlated with tangible success in professional contexts.

Better relationships. Improving your empathy, communication and emotional awareness will help you forge and maintain personal and professional relationships. This in turn helps you in your career and with sustaining happiness in your personal life.

Developing a Toolkit

Improving emotional intelligence is about developing a set of techniques that can help your awareness and response to your emotions and other people. Some of these techniques include:

  • Meditation. Meditation improves your focus, reduces anxiety and helps you become aware of what is happening both internally and externally.
  • Journaling. Writing down what happens to you and how you felt about it helps you track things that trigger your emotions and reflect on how you responded to them.
  • Active listening. Listening needs to be practised like anything else. Improving your listening helps your communication which helps you to establish and maintain relationships.
